The Story Behind This

Two months ago I didn't know what lacrosse was. At CMSAC, Dean Oliver mentioned that lacrosse should have its own dataset and more people should be doing serious analysis on it. When an opportunity came with Virginia Athletics Women's Lacrosse, I jumped.

The initial brief: "Tell us something with the data. Momentum shifts. Impact of each play." When I asked where the data lived, I was handed PDFs of season stats and pointed to the NCAA website.

I reached out to Zach Cippucci (founder of Lacrosse Reference) and started building. Two months later, we have a full scouting-to-analysis pipeline.

March 10 message to coaches: "Virginia 12, Princeton 10. Close game decided by draws and first-half execution." — The prediction survived a game-ending 3-minute window where a goal was waved off, a shot hit the post at 1:17, and a free position attempt was saved.

At halftime, down 3–6, the analysis identified the root problem: ISO lacrosse into Hughes' comfort zone. Recommendation: ball movement, wing attacks, off-ball cuts. Virginia outscored Princeton 4–0 in Q3 to retake the lead.
