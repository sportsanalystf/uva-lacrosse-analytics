What happened: Princeton led 11-9 with 13:34 remaining. Virginia scored 4 unanswered goals to win 13-11. DiNardo scored the game-winner. Galica had 3G/1A/12 DCs. Foster had a hat trick. MacDonald had 3G/2A for Princeton. Morrisroe scored 2G.

What changes now: Virginia has home-field advantage (2025 was at Princeton). Princeton lost McKenzie Blake (89G, Ivy ATT of the Year) and Sophie Whiteway (249 career DCs). But Morrisroe has doubled her production and Bacigalupo has emerged. Princeton's draw deficit is worse without Whiteway. Virginia's draw advantage is LARGER than last year.

OFFENSIVE PLAN vs PRINCETON DEFENSE — OC

1. Quick ball movement to create unsettled looks. Princeton runs zone looks. Fast reversals and off-ball cuts designed to shift the zone are the best counter. Alaimo's elite passing (2.67 APG) should find seams.

2. Attack from the wings. Hughes sets center-cage. Wing shots and crease feeds beat her positioning. DiNardo and Foster should attack from the flanks, not top-center.

3. Capitalize on draw control advantage. Push tempo immediately off every Galica draw win. Princeton cannot defend in transition — they need time to set their zone. Get shots before they organize.

4. Target 12+ goals. Hughes will save 10+. We need 30+ SOG to score 12-14. Accept that she'll make big saves — maintain volume.

DEFENSIVE PLAN vs PRINCETON OFFENSE — DC

1. Deny Dora's passing lanes. She is the primary facilitator. Station a defender in her primary passing lane (top of the 8m to crease). If Dora can't distribute, Princeton's connected offense fractures into individual drives.

2. Shadow Morrisroe with dedicated defender. She is scoring 4.0 GPG. Do NOT let her get comfortable in Q1/Q2. Physical, tight coverage. She elevates in H2 — maintain intensity for 60 minutes.

3. Force turnovers in transition. Princeton's draw deficit means they have fewer possessions. Every turnover we force is magnified. Aggressive ride on clearing — their clearing % is elite (.959 in 2025) so we need to disrupt early.

4. Pack the crease in settled. Princeton's motion offense creates crease feeds. Deny the crease feed and force outside shots. Hughes will handle some of the 12m attempts — let her.

DRAW CONTROL PLAN — GALICA vs PRINCETON'S COMMITTEE

This is the game's decisive matchup. Princeton lost Whiteway (249 career DCs) and is now using a committee: Dora (7 DCs at Harvard), Abigail Roberts, Quinn, Bacigalupo, and freshman Kirschner. They've been DOMINATED on draws: 6-20 vs Loyola, 8-16 vs Rutgers, 8-21 vs Maryland. Harvard improved but still unclear.

Plan: Galica exclusive draw taker. She had 12 DCs vs Princeton in 2025 (when they had Whiteway). Without Whiteway, this mismatch is even larger. Target: 60%+ of draws (13+ of ~22).

GALICA 12+ DCS = VIRGINIA WIN PROBABILITY 70%+. This is the single biggest swing factor.

⚔ IN-GAME DECISION TREE

IF Virginia leads 4+ at half: Maintain defensive structure. Princeton WILL make a second-half push. Keep Morrisroe shadowed. Run full possessions to eat clock.

IF Princeton rallies to within 2 in Q3: Timeout. Sub fresh defensive legs. Push for a Galica draw win → fast-break goal to restore cushion.

IF MacDonald has 3+ goals by halftime: She's in the zone (200th career point momentum). Double her on settled looks, force Bacigalupo/Sloan to beat us instead.

IF game is tied or UVA trails entering Q4: DANGER ZONE. Princeton is a Q4 team (5G vs Loyola, ~6G vs Harvard in Q4). Push tempo, win draws, score first in Q4.
