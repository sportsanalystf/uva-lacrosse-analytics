# Virginia Women's Lacrosse — Post-Game Analysis
## Virginia 12, Princeton 10 | March 11, 2026 | Klöckner Stadium, Charlottesville | Non-Conference — W

---

## 1. Summary

This was a game we had no business nearly losing — and a game that exposed exactly who we are right now: a team with elite draw control and individual talent that can look like two completely different squads within the same 60 minutes.

We scored first (39 seconds in), led 3-2 after Q1 thanks to 6 draw wins, then went SCORELESS in Q2 while Princeton rattled off 4 unanswered to take a 6-3 halftime lead. The second quarter was the worst 15 minutes of lacrosse we've played since the Stanford collapse — 9 shots, 0 goals, and Amelia Hughes standing on her head with 6 saves while we fired ISO shots from distance with no ball movement.

Then the Q3 switch flipped. We shut Princeton out 4-0, retook the lead 7-6, and survived a wild Q4 (5-4) to win 12-10. Kate Galica was the difference — 5 goals, 11 draw controls, and the two clutch man-up goals in Q4 that broke the 9-9 tie.

### What decided the game:

- Three things:

  - First, **draw control dominance** — 19-3 is the most lopsided draw battle of the entire season. Princeton simply couldn't get the ball. We had 41 shots to their 21 because of this. But having the ball means nothing if you can't finish, which leads to...

  - Second, **the Q2-to-Q3 offensive transformation.** In Q2, we played ISO lacrosse — Dinardo forcing shots from distance (4 saves by Hughes on his attempts), Galica shooting into traffic, Alaimo turning it over twice before getting carded. In Q3, we moved the ball: Galica combined with Allen and Alaimo, we attacked gaps instead of shooters, and scored 4 on 5 SOG. The coaching adjustment at halftime — or the team's own reset — was the game.

  - Third, **Galica's Q4 takeover.** Down 9-9 after Sloan's MU/FPG, Galica scored back-to-back man-up goals (6:52 FPG, 5:14 with Alaimo assist) to make it 11-9. Then forced a Princeton clear failure at 1:26 and grabbed her own rebound off the post at 1:17. She willed the win.


### Was this a process win or results win?

- Results win. The process was badly flawed for 15 minutes (Q2) and shaky for portions of Q4.

  - The good process: 19-3 draws, 6 turnovers (season-low), 100% clearing (11-11), 41 shots generated, Q3 shutout.

  - The bad process: 0-for-Q2 offensively (ISO ball, no movement, shooting from distance into Hughes), 3 green cards (Flaherty Q1, Alaimo Q2, Laverghetta Q4 — all led to Princeton goals or opportunities), Princeton's 10 goals on 15 SOG (66.7% conversion = our defense was leaking), Finnelle's 5 saves / 33.3% (sharp regression from back-to-back 58.8% games).

  - The persistent issues: Alaimo's turnovers (4 — back up from 2 vs FSU), Dinardo's ball distribution (throws it away rather than finding the open player), and defensive breakdowns on Princeton's passing game.

## 2. Game Flow & Momentum Analysis

### First 5 Minutes

Strong start — Reilly won the opening draw, Dinardo scored at 14:21 → 1-0 (39 seconds in). Second straight game scoring first. But then Alaimo immediately turned it over, Flaherty got a green card at 13:18, and Sloan converted the man-up at 12:41 → 1-1. Pattern: score, self-inflict. Allen restored the lead at 10:10 → 2-1 off a MacDonald turnover forced by Reilly. Good response.

### The Q2 Disaster (0-4)

This was the critical failure. Nine shots, zero goals. Hughes saved everything — Sfreddo (11:30), Alaimo FP (8:11), Dinardo (7:10), Dinardo FP (5:22), Galica (3:32), Galica (0:47). The problem wasn't shot volume — it was **shot quality.** We were shooting from distance, going ISO, trying to beat Hughes from the top of the arc rather than moving the ball and attacking from the wings or crease. Hughes was set, square, and ready on every shot because our shooters were telegraphing from the same angles.

Meanwhile, Princeton scored 4: Sloan MU/FPG (9:18, off Alaimo's green card), Morrisroe (6:22, Sloan assist), MacDonald (2:42, Dora assist), Bacigalupo (2:02, Dora assist). The last two came 40 seconds apart — Haven Dora had 3 assists in Q2 and Princeton's ball movement was everything ours wasn't. Quick passes, diagonal cuts, exploiting our late slides. Timeout called at 2:02 (down 3-6). WP cratered from ~68% to ~18%.

### The Q3 Transformation (4-0)

Whatever was said at halftime worked. The offense completely changed:

- **Ball movement replaced ISO.** Galica's goal at 4:53 came off an Alaimo assist — ball was reversed twice before the final pass. The Allen-to-Galica goal at 11:45 involved off-ball movement that created space.

- **Shot placement improved.** Instead of blasting from distance, we attacked closer and placed shots. Flaherty's FPG at 12:21 was composed. Dinardo's goal at 1:18 came after patient possession.

- **Defense locked in.** Princeton had 4 shots, 0 goals. Finnelle saved Sloan (13:36) and Quinn (9:21). Finnelle also caused a Dora turnover at 6:30. The back line was tighter and communication improved.

Four unanswered goals. WP rose from ~18% to ~72%. This Q3 performance mirrors the Pitt Q3 (5-1) and FSU Q3 (5-2) — we are a third-quarter team. The question remains: why can't we play like this for 60 minutes?

### Q4 Rollercoaster

Allen → 8-6. Bacigalupo → 8-7. Piraino (Alaimo) → 9-7. Bacigalupo (Sloan) → 9-8. Then: Laverghetta green card at 9:14 + Kology foul → Sloan MU/FPG at 8:16 → **9-9**. Princeton timeout.

The crisis moment. WP dropped to ~42%. This team has been here before — down 6-7 vs Notre Dame Q4, down 9-7 vs Pitt Q4. Both times, we responded. This time:

Galica MU/FPG at 6:52 → 10-9 (off Roberts yellow card). Galica MU (Alaimo) at 5:14 → 11-9. Morrisroe cut it to 11-10 at 4:11. But Piraino sealed it at 3:17 (Alaimo assist) → 12-10. Then Galica forced a failed Princeton clear at 1:26, hit post at 1:17, grabbed her own rebound. Clock ran out. Win.

### Did We Dictate or React?

Q1: Dictated draws (6-0) but reacted to our own cards. Q2: Completely reactive — Princeton dictated everything. Q3: Dictated fully — 4-0 shutout. Q4: Mixed — traded blows, needed Galica to take over individually.

## 3. Offensive Analysis

### Shot Volume vs Quality — The Core Issue

41 shots is the most we've taken all season. But 27 SOG on 41 attempts (65.9% accuracy) is our worst. And 12 goals on 27 SOG (44.4% conversion) is below our recent standard (68.2% vs FSU, 52.6% vs Pitt). Hughes' 15 saves (55.6%) tell the story — we made her job easy in Q1-Q2 by shooting predictably.

**The Q2 problem in detail:** 9 shots, 6 on goal (66.7% accuracy), 0 goals (0% conversion). Hughes saved all 6. Film review shows: all 6 saves came from shots taken centrally or from the top of the arc. Zero shots from the crease, zero shots from the wings. We were playing ISO and letting Hughes see everything. Dinardo in particular took 4 of the 9 Q2 shots — all from the same angle, all saved or wide.

**The Q3-Q4 solution:** 23 shots, 15 on goal, 9 goals (60% conversion on SOG). The difference: ball movement. Galica's Q3 goals involved assists (Allen, Alaimo). We attacked different angles. We took shots from closer range after dodges created space.

## 4. Defensive Analysis

### The Problem: Princeton's Passing

Princeton scored 10 goals on only 15 shots on goal — 66.7% conversion rate. That's the highest any opponent has converted against us this season. The issue wasn't Princeton's individual talent — it was their team passing. Haven Dora had 4 assists, Ella Sloan had 3 assists (plus 3 goals). They moved the ball faster than our defense could rotate.

The Q2 sequence at 2:42-2:02 was the worst example: MacDonald scored (Dora assist), then Bacigalupo scored 40 seconds later (Dora assist). Zero defensive reset between goals. Our defenders were ball-watching, not tracking cutters.

### Matchup Assessment

**vs Ella Sloan (3G-3A = 6PTS):** Lost badly. She was their engine — scored on MU/FPG situations and created for others with 3 assists. All 3 of her goals came on man-up or FP situations created by our fouls.

**vs Maggie Bacigalupo (3G):** Lost. All 3 goals came from the crease area off assists (MacDonald Q2, Dora Q2, Sloan Q4). She exploited late slides every time.

**vs Haven Dora (4A):** Lost the chess match. Dora never shot but orchestrated 4 of 10 Princeton goals. She found gaps in our defense repeatedly — especially in Q2 where she had 3 assists.

### Caused Turnovers

7 caused turnovers: Reilly 1 (MacDonald Q1), Schneider 1 (MacDonald Q1), Demark 1 (Dora Q2), Alaimo 1 (Dylan Allen Q2), Finnelle 1 (Dora Q3), White 1 (Sloan Q3), Galica 1 (Dylan Allen Q4). Better than recent games — but our defense is generating turnovers through individual effort, not system pressure.

## 9. Film Room Emphasis Plan

### 5 Clips We Must Show

1. **Q2 vs Q3 Side-by-Side.** Show Q2 ISO shooting (Dinardo from distance, Galica into traffic, no passes) next to Q3 ball movement (Allen-to-Galica, Alaimo-to-Galica, off-ball cuts). Same team, same opponent, completely different offense. Ask: "What changed?"

2. **Princeton's Q2 Passing Game (Dora's 3 assists).** Show Dora finding Morrisroe, MacDonald, and Bacigalupo in sequence. Identify where our slides were late and where cutters were left free. This is what good teams do to us.

3. **The 9-9 Crisis Sequence.** Laverghetta green card (9:14) → Kology foul → Sloan MU/FPG (8:16) → Princeton timeout → Galica MU/FPG (6:52) → Galica MU (5:14). Show the cost of the card AND the response. Both lessons matter.

4. **Hughes' 6 Q2 Saves.** Show where each shot was placed. Every one came from central/top-arc position. Show what happens when you shoot into the goalie's strength zone vs when you attack wings and low corners (Q3-Q4).

5. **Dinardo's 10 Shots.** Show all 10. 4 wide, 4 saved, 2 goals. He's forcing shots when covered. When he sees a clean look, he finishes. When he doesn't, he fires anyway. He needs the pass option drilled.

### 3 Clips We Celebrate

1. **Galica's Q4 Takeover.** FPG at 6:52, MU goal at 5:14, forced turnover at 1:26, hit post and own rebound at 1:17. Four plays. That's a closer mentality.

2. **Q3 4-0 Shutout.** Full quarter. This is the identity when we're locked in.

3. **Piraino's 12-10 Dagger at 3:17.** Alaimo assist, clean finish. Insurance goal when Princeton was creeping back. Composure.

### 1 Culture Moment

**The halftime reset.** Down 3-6, going into the locker room after a scoreless quarter, and coming out to score 4 unanswered. That's character. That's growth. Five games ago (Stanford), this team would have folded. Today, they fought back from 3 goals down. Show Q3 opening → Dinardo draw win → Flaherty FPG → Galica goal. "This is what we look like when we stop playing for ourselves and start playing for each other."

## Bottom Line

A ugly win is still a win. Virginia 12, Princeton 10 — but this game exposed both the best and worst of this team.

**The best:** 19-3 draw dominance (Galica 11). Season-low 6 turnovers. 100% clearing. Kate Galica's 5-goal, 11-draw masterclass. A 4-0 Q3 shutout comeback from 3-6 down. Clutch Q4 man-up execution.

**The worst:** A scoreless Q2 where we played ISO lacrosse and let Hughes save 6 shots. Princeton scoring 10 on 15 SOG because our defense couldn't handle their passing. Three green cards that directly produced 3 Princeton man-up goals. Finnelle's save rate dropping to 33.3%. Alaimo's turnovers back up to 4.

**The trajectory:**

| Metric | Stanford | Pitt | FSU | Princeton | Direction |
|--------|----------|------|-----|-----------|-----------|
| Turnovers | 15 | 16 | 13 | 6 | ✅ Improving |
| Draw Win % | 63% | 68% | 67% | 86% | ✅ Dominant |
| Cards | 4 | 6 | 1 | 3 | ⚠️ Inconsistent |
| Finnelle SV% | 33% | 59% | 59% | 33% | ⚠️ Regression |
| Alaimo TOs | 8 | 1 | 2 | 4 | ⚠️ Regression |
| Goals Scored | 8 | 10 | 15 | 12 | ✅ Trending up |

We're 5-4. The draw circle is elite. The offense has weapons. The defense needs to solve passing teams. And we need to stop giving opponents man-up goals by carding ourselves at critical moments.

The Q2 lesson is the most important one from this game: **when we play ISO, we lose. When we move the ball, we win.** The entire offensive game plan going forward should be built around that truth.
